📚 Chicago 17th Edition — Complete Style Guide

The Chicago Manual of Style (17th edition) is one of the oldest and most comprehensive style guides in academic publishing. It offers two citation systems: Notes-Bibliography (N-B) for humanities and Author-Date (A-D) for sciences and social sciences.

Notes-Bibliography System (Humanities)

Used in history, literature, arts, and theology. Citations appear as numbered footnotes or endnotes, with a full bibliography at the end.

Footnote — Book¹ John A. Smith, The History of Legal Systems (Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2024), 45.
Bibliography — BookSmith, John A. The History of Legal Systems. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2024.

Author-Date System (Sciences)

In-Text Citation(Smith 2024, 45)
Reference ListSmith, John A. 2024. The History of Legal Systems. Oxford: Oxford University Press.

Turabian vs Chicago

Turabian (A Manual for Writers of Research Papers, Theses, and Dissertations) is based on Chicago and is designed specifically for student papers. It is slightly simplified compared to the full Chicago Manual but follows the same N-B and A-D structures.
