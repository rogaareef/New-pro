🏛️ Harvard Citation Style — Complete Guide

The Harvard referencing style (also called author-date or parenthetical referencing) is one of the most widely used citation systems in the world. It is the standard in many UK, Australian, and international universities, particularly in health sciences, medicine, natural sciences, and business.

In-Text Citation Format

Basic Format(Author surname, Year) or Author surname (Year)
With Page Number(Smith, 2024, p. 45) or (Smith, 2024, pp. 45-47)
Two Authors(Smith and Jones, 2024)
Three or More Authors(Smith et al., 2024)

Reference List Formats

Journal ArticleSmith, J.A. and Jones, M.B. (2024) 'Artificial intelligence in clinical decision support', British Medical Journal, 385(4), pp. 1234-1241. doi: 10.1136/bmj.2024.001.
BookJohnson, C.D. (2023) Advanced Research Methods in Health Sciences. 3rd edn. London: Sage Publications.

Harvard vs APA — Key Differences

  • Harvard uses full first year in parentheses:(Smith, 2024)· APA uses the same
  • Harvard title format varies by institution — some capitalize all words, others capitalize only the first
  • Harvard has no single definitive manual — institutions publish their own guidelines; APA has a standardized manual
