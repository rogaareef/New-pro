📖 Complete APA 7th Edition Guide

The 7th edition of the APA Publication Manual (2020) is the current standard for academic writing in psychology, social sciences, education, nursing, business, and many other disciplines. It introduced significant changes from the 6th edition that every researcher must know.

Key Changes in APA 7th Edition

  • Running head removed — no longer required for student papers
  • Up to 20 authors listed in the reference list before using an ellipsis
  • DOI as hyperlink — format is now https://doi.org/xxxxxx
  • New student paper format with different title page requirements
  • "they" as singular pronoun now officially recognized
  • Publisher location removed from book references

APA 7 — Journal Article Format

FormatAuthor, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
ExampleSmith, J. A., & Johnson, M. B. (2024). Artificial intelligence in legal research: A systematic review. Journal of Legal Technology, 12(3), 88–117. https://doi.org/10.1234/jlt.2024.001

APA 7 — Book Format

FormatAuthor, A. A. (Year). Title of book: Subtitle if any. Publisher.
ExampleBrown, C. D. (2023). Advanced research methods in law and social science. Oxford University Press.

APA 7 — Website Format

FormatAuthor, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name. URL
ExampleWorld Health Organization. (2024, March 15). Mental health fact sheet. WHO. https://www.who.int/mental_health

APA 7 — Dissertation Format

FormatAuthor, A. A. (Year). Title of dissertation [Doctoral dissertation, University Name]. Database Name. URL
ExampleAl-Hassan, M. R. (2024). AI-powered legal research tools: Efficacy and adoption in MENA law firms [Doctoral dissertation, Alexandria University]. ProQuest Dissertations.
